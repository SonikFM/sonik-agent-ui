Stateful Runtimes Landscape (July 2026)

Nothing shipping in July 2026 combines workflows-as-validated-JSON, one generic interpreter, and a typed host-signed approval boundary in a single package. The scan ratifies keeping our hand-rolled Decider reducer and names the substrate, the candidates, and the ruled-out field.

Source: docs/research/stateful-runtimes-landscape-2026-07.md · Corpus 2026-07-07 · Status updated 2026-07-11

Headline finding

The lens is fixed: a TypeScript monorepo on Cloudflare Workers, a Zod-validated pure-reducer state machine (workflow-run-state.ts), workflows carried as JSON documents and executed by one generic controller, host-signed approval gates, and semantic receipts. Measured against that lens, the open-source ecosystem has close cousins but no match.

No engine ships all three of these together

(a) workflows as validated JSON documents, (b) one generic interpreter over them, (c) a typed, host-verified approval boundary. The nearest neighbours each hold exactly one: Netflix/Orkes Conductor is data-as-workflow but has no trust model and is Java; Pydantic AI has typed approval payloads but no durability and is Python; Restate has durable-promise approval gates but runs an external server.

Our reducer is not an ad-hoc wheel. decide(command, state) → events with rejection expressed as data and effects injected is the Decider pattern as formalized by Emmett and fmodel-ts — a named, recognized shape. The pattern is shipped and green:

SHIPPED pure reducer, host-signed approval structurally required (model_supplied_approval_is_not_trusted), phase committed only on semantic-success receipts. The typed host-signed approval boundary remains uncommoditized across every project below — it is the moat.

The decisive filter is the edge

Only Cloudflare Workflows/DO and Restate have a real Workers story; DBOS and Resonate are library-shaped enough to embed. Every full platform (Temporal, Inngest, Hatchet, Trigger.dev, Windmill) fails on "None" for Workers before HITL maturity even enters the argument.

Mastra: the road not taken

Mastra (TS, 1.0 in Jan 2026, $22M Series A in Apr 2026, ~300k weekly downloads) is the agent framework this project would most plausibly have adopted. Its stateSchema is structurally close to our reducer state. Three findings cut against it, and a fourth — churn — is disqualifying on its own.

  • No workflow-as-data
    Code-first .then() / .branch() only. The vNext direction is stronger code-first typing, not documents. Our entire model is workflows as validated JSON executed by one interpreter; Mastra structurally cannot host that.
  • No approval authority model
    resume() trusts any shape-valid payload — no identity, no signing, no audit primitive. This is precisely the boundary we treat as the moat, and Mastra has none of it.
  • Snapshot format explicitly internal and unversioned
    An unsafe interop target. We cannot persist or exchange state through a format the vendor reserves the right to change silently.

Four breaking renames of core primitives in one month, no semver guarantee on the stable channel

Run, session, and HITL primitives were renamed four times between 12 Jun and 6 Jul 2026. Two of the renames landed within two days of each other. A dependency this central cannot churn its vocabulary at this rate.

Mastra's Cloudflare deployer is real (D1/KV/DO adapters), but the docs steer to DO for consistency because KV is an eventually-consistent footgun and D1 has a hard 10GB cap — a reminder that even the shipped edge path carries caveats.
